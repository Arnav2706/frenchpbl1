Biography

Simon Vouet (1590â€“1649) was a key figure in French Baroque art. After spending 15 years in Italy, where he was deeply influenced by the Baroque style, Vouet returned to France and became court painter for Louis XIII. His work is characterized by dramatic lighting, vivid colors, and dynamic compositions.

Artistic Contributions

Vouet was instrumental in introducing the Baroque style to France. His works blended Italian influences with a distinct French flair, setting the stage for later artists like Charles Le Brun. He created numerous religious and allegorical works that reflected the grandeur and emotion of the Baroque period.
